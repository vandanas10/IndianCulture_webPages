Makarsankranti

Makar Sankranti is a Hindu festival that marks the transition of the Sun into the zodiac sign of Capricorn (Makar Rashi). It is celebrated on January 14th or 15th every year, depending on the Hindu calendar. The festival is known by different names in different parts of India and is celebrated with great enthusiasm, usually involving kite flying, bonfires, and feasting. It signifies the onset of longer days and is considered an auspicious time for new beginnings.

Lohri

Lohri is a winter festival celebrated primarily in the northern states of India, especially Punjab. It marks the end of the winter solstice and the beginning of longer days. The festival is celebrated with bonfires, singing and dancing around the fire, offering prayers, and sharing traditional food items such as rewari, gajak, and peanuts. Lohri is a time for reuniting with family and friends and spreading joy and happiness.

Basant Panchami

Basant Panchami is a Hindu festival that marks the arrival of spring. It is celebrated on the fifth day (Panchami) of the Hindu month of Magha (usually in late January or early February). The festival is dedicated to the goddess Saraswati, who represents knowledge, wisdom, music, and learning. People wear yellow clothes, offer prayers, and fly kites to celebrate the occasion. It is especially significant for students and is considered an auspicious day to start learning something new.

Republic Day

Indian Republic Day is celebrated on 26th January every year, marking the day when the Constitution of India came into effect in 1950. The day is commemorated with parades, flag-hoisting ceremonies, and cultural programs across the country. It is a day to celebrate the rich diversity and unity of India, and to remember the sacrifices of the freedom fighters who fought for the country's independence.

Pongal

Pongal is a four-day harvest festival celebrated in Tamil Nadu. The first day, involves the burning of old items to symbolize new beginnings. The second day, is dedicated to the Sun God and is marked by preparing sweet rice pudding called Pongal kheer. The dish is cooked in a pot until it overflows, symbolizing abundance and prosperity. The third day, is for celebrating cattle, and the fourth day, involves family gatherings and outings.

Maha Shivratri

Maha Shivratri is a significant Hindu festival dedicated to Lord Shiva, one of the principal deities of Hinduism. It's celebrated with great fervor and devotion across India through fasting, meditation, and offerings of fruits and flowers to Lord Shiva. It's believed that observing fast and performing prayer and meditation on Maha Shivratri can bestow spiritual upliftment and liberation from ignorance.

Baisakhi

Baisakhi, also known as Vaisakhi, is a festival celebrated by the Sikh community on 13th April every year. It marks the establishment of the Khalsa Panth by Guru Gobind Singh in 1699. The day is observed with colorful processions, kirtans, and feasts, signifying the spirit of community and equality. Baisakhi also marks the beginning of the harvest season in Punjab.

Holi

Holi is a vibrant and colorful festival celebrated in India and other parts of the world with great enthusiasm. It marks the beginning of spring and celebrates the triumph of good over evil. People celebrate by smearing colored powder on each other, throwing water balloons, dancing to music, and feasting on traditional sweets and snacks. It's a time to forget differences and come together to enjoy the spirit of love and friendship.

Mahavir Jayanti

Mahavir Jayanti is a Jain festival that celebrates the birth anniversary of Lord Mahavir, the 24th Tirthankara (spiritual teacher) of the Jains. It falls on the 13th day of the Hindu month of Chaitra (usually in March or April). Devotees visit Jain temples, offer prayers, and perform charitable acts to mark the occasion. Lord Mahavir's teachings of non-violence, compassion, and truth are central to the festival's significance.

Bihu

Bihu is a set of three harvest festivals celebrated in Assam, India: Rongali Bihu, Kongali Bihu, and Bhogali Bihu. Rongali Bihu, also known as Bohag Bihu, marks the beginning of the Assamese new year in mid-April. The festivals are observed with traditional dance, music, and feasting, celebrating the abundance of the harvest season and the spirit of community.

Thrissur Pooram

Thrissur Pooram is a vibrant and grand festival celebrated in Kerala, India, in the month of April or May. The festival is a display of 30 ornately decorated elephants and their mahouts, along with colorful umbrellas and percussion music. The festivities last for 36 hours and conclude with a fireworks display. Thrissur Pooram is a celebration of culture, tradition, and unity.

Buddha Jayanti

Buddha Jayanti, also known as Vesak or Buddha Purnima, is a significant festival celebrated by Buddhists around the world in honor of the birth, enlightenment, and death of Gautama Buddha. The day is marked with prayer, meditation, and offerings of flowers, symbolizing peace, harmony, and compassion. Buddha Jayanti is a celebration of the Buddha's teachings and their universal relevance.

Losar

Losar is a Tibetan Buddhist festival that marks the Tibetan New Year. It is celebrated in Tibet, Nepal, Bhutan, and among Tibetan communities in other parts of the world. The festival falls in February or March and is observed with prayers, feasting, and cultural events. It symbolizes the victory of good over evil and the start of a new year filled with happiness and prosperity.

Mewar

Mewar festival is an annual celebration of the vibrant culture and heritage of the Mewar region in Rajasthan, India. It is celebrated in the month of March or April, during the Hindu festival of Gangaur. The festival is marked by colorful processions, cultural displays, traditional music and dance performances, and competitions. Tourists and locals alike flock to Udaipur to experience the lively atmosphere and take part in the festivities.

Eid-ul-Fitr

Eid ul Fitr, also known as the "Festival of Breaking the Fast," is an important Islamic holiday celebrated by Muslims worldwide. The festival marks the end of the month-long dawn-to-sunset fasting during Ramadan. The day is celebrated with prayer, family gatherings, and feasting, signifying the importance of charity, forgiveness, and unity within the Muslim community. Eid ul Fitr is a joyous occasion that promotes spirituality, generosity, and gratitude.

Rath Yatra

Rath Yatra, also known as the "Festival of Chariots," is a significant Hindu festival celebrated in Puri, Odisha, India. The festival involves the procession of three huge chariots carrying idols of Lord Jagannath, his brother Balabhadra, and sister Subhadra. The chariots are pulled by devotees, and the festival is marked by music, dance, and feasting. Rath Yatra is a celebration of the victory of good over evil and emphasizes the importance of devotion, unity, and harmony.

Ugadi

Ugadi is a new year festival celebrated in the southern states of India, particularly in Andhra Pradesh, Telangana, and Karnataka. It falls on the first day of the Hindu month of Chaitra (usually in March or April). The festival signifies the beginning of a new era and is celebrated with traditional rituals, feasting, and decorations. It is believed that the festival brings good luck and prosperity.

Gudi Padwa

Ugadi is a new year festival celebrated in the southern states of India, particularly in Andhra Pradesh, Telangana, and Karnataka. It falls on the first day of the Hindu month of Chaitra (usually in March or April). The festival signifies the beginning of a new era and is celebrated with traditional rituals, feasting, and decorations. It is believed that the festival brings good luck and prosperity.

Kumbh Mela

Kumbh Mela is a major pilgrimage festival for Hindus held every 12 years (or every 6 years for the Ardh Kumbh) at four different locations in India. The festival involves a holy dip in a sacred river, believed to wash away sins and purify the soul. Millions of people from different parts of India and abroad gather to take part in the festival, making it the largest peaceful gathering of humans on the planet. The event also involves various religious rituals, discourses, and cultural events.

Hemis

Hemis is an annual festival celebrated at Hemis Monastery in Ladakh, India, to commemorate the birth anniversary of Guru Padmasambhava, the founder of Tibetan Buddhism. The festival features colorful masked dances, music, and rituals, attracting visitors from all over the world. Hemis is a celebration of spirituality, culture, and tradition and showcases the unique identity of Ladakhi Buddhist culture.

Raksha Bandhan

Raksha Bandhan is a Hindu festival celebrated to honor the bond between brothers and sisters. Sisters tie a sacred thread called a "rakhi" on their brothers' wrists, signifying the bond of love and protection. The brothers, in turn, promise to protect and take care of their sisters. The festival is marked with prayer, gift-giving, and feasting, emphasizing the importance of family, love, and harmony. Raksha Bandhan is a celebration of the unique and special bond between siblings.

Krishna Janmashtmi

Krishna Janmashtami is a Hindu festival celebrated to mark the birth anniversary of Lord Krishna. The festival is marked with prayer, fasting, and devotional singing. Devotees offer flowers, fruits, and sweets to the deity and engage in lively celebrations that include dance, music, and drama. Janmashtami emphasizes the importance of devotion, love, and compassion and is a celebration of the divine qualities of Lord Krishna.

Independence Day

Independence Day in India is celebrated every year on August 15 to mark the nation's independence from British colonial rule in 1947. The day is marked with flag hoisting, patriotic speeches, parades, and cultural programs. It is a time for reflection on India's rich cultural heritage and its struggles for freedom, emphasizing the importance of unity, diversity, and national pride.

Onam

Onam is a ten-day-long harvest festival celebrated in Kerala, India. The festival is marked by vibrant cultural programs, boat races, feasting, and the creation of floral carpets known as "pookalam." Onam celebrates the return of the mythical King Mahabali and emphasizes the importance of hospitality, community, and cultural traditions. It is a time for families to come together, share meals, and celebrate the bounty of nature.

Ganesh Chaturthi

Ganesh Chaturthi is a Hindu festival celebrated to honor Lord Ganesha, the elephant-headed god. The festival is marked with the installation of Ganesha idols in homes and public places, prayer, and devotional singing. The festival culminates with the immersion of the idols in water bodies, symbolizing the return of Lord Ganesha to his heavenly abode. Ganesh Chaturthi is a celebration of new beginnings, prosperity, and the removal of obstacles.

Navratri

Navratri is a nine-day-long Hindu festival celebrated to honor the divine feminine energy or Shakti. The festival is marked with fasting, prayer, and the worship of Goddess Durga, who represents the triumph of good over evil. Navratri culminates with Dussehra, the celebration of the victory of Lord Rama over the demon king Ravana. Navratri is a celebration of feminine power, perseverance, and triumph over darkness.

Durga Puja

Durga Puja is a major Hindu festival celebrated primarily in West Bengal, India, and among Bengali communities worldwide. It celebrates the victory of goddess Durga over the demon Mahishasura. The festival involves the installation of elaborately decorated pandals (temporary structures) and idols of Durga, offering prayers and performing cultural events. The festival is also a time for feasting and reconnecting with friends and family.

Dussehra

Dussehra is a major Hindu festival that celebrates the victory of good over evil. It marks the end of the nine-day festival of Navratri and commemorates the triumph of Lord Rama over the demon king Ravana. The festival is celebrated with great enthusiasm across India with the burning of effigies of Ravana and fireworks. It is also a time for reconnecting with friends and family, feasting, and exchanging gifts.

Diwali

Diwali, also known as the festival of lights, is one of the biggest Hindu festivals celebrated worldwide. It symbolizes the victory of light over darkness and good over evil. The festival is marked by lighting diyas (lamps), bursting fireworks, offering prayers, exchanging sweets and gifts, and enjoying traditional delicacies. It is a time for reuniting with family and friends, spreading love and joy, and welcoming a new beginning.

Chhath Puja

Chhath Puja is a Hindu festival celebrated primarily in the states of Bihar, Jharkhand, and Uttar Pradesh, India. It is dedicated to the worship of the Sun God and his consorts Usha and Pratyusha. The festival involves offering prayers, fasting, and performing rituals on the banks of a water body, usually a river or a lake. It is celebrated with great devotion and enthusiasm and is believed to bring prosperity and happiness to the devotees.

Gurupurab

Gurupurab is a Sikh festival that commemorates the birth anniversary of Guru Nanak, the founder of Sikhism. The festival is celebrated with great fervor and enthusiasm by Sikhs worldwide. The celebrations involve offering prayers, performing religious rituals, reading and singing hymns from the Sikh scriptures, and serving langar (community meal). The festival promotes the values of selfless service, equality, and compassion that Guru Nanak espoused.

Hornbill

The Hornbill Festival is a cultural festival celebrated in Nagaland, India. It is named after the hornbill bird, which is revered by the Naga people. The festival is a celebration of Naga culture, art, and music, and is marked by colorful displays, traditional dances, and music performances. The festival is also an opportunity for visitors to experience the rich diversity of Naga tribes and their way of life.

Christmas

Christmas is an annual Christian festival that celebrates the birth of Jesus Christ. The festival is marked by the decoration of homes and public spaces with lights, ornaments, and trees. It is a time for gift-giving, feasting, singing carols, attending church services, and spending time with family and friends. Christmas promotes the values of love, kindness, and goodwill towards all.
