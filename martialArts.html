Kalaripayattu

Kalaripayattu is an ancient Indian martial art originating in Kerala. It involves a combination of strikes, kicks, grappling, and weaponry, and is known for its fluidity and grace. Kalaripayattu is considered one of the oldest martial arts in the world and has influenced other martial arts such as karate and kung fu.

Gatka

Gatka is a traditional martial art form from Punjab, India, that is based on the use of wooden sticks and swords. It is considered an important part of Sikh culture and is often performed during Sikh festivals. Gatka involves both offensive and defensive techniques and emphasizes discipline and mental focus.

Thang-ta

Thang-ta is a martial art form from the Indian state of Manipur that combines sword fighting with dance movements. It involves the use of swords, spears, and other weapons, and emphasizes grace and fluidity. Thang-ta has a long history in Manipur and is an important part of the state's cultural heritage.

Silambam

Silambam is a traditional Indian martial art form from the southern state of Tamil Nadu. It involves the use of a long staff or bamboo stick and emphasizes fast-paced movements and intricate footwork. Silambam is considered a weapon-based martial art and has influenced other martial arts such as kalaripayattu and eskrima.

Malla-yuddha

Malla-yuddha, also known as Indian wrestling, is a traditional form of wrestling that has been practiced in India for centuries. It involves grappling and throwing techniques and is often performed during festivals and competitions. Malla-yuddha emphasizes physical strength and endurance and has a long history in Indian culture.
